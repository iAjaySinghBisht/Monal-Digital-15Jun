People imagine animation as one big creative act. It's actually a relay race,
and the baton gets dropped at the handoffs. Here's how we keep it moving.

## Boards are the cheapest place to fail

A storyboard is where a sequence is at its most flexible and its least
expensive. We are ruthless here, cutting scenes, re-timing jokes, and re-staging
shots, because every problem solved on a board costs a hundredth of what it
costs after animation.

## The middle stages nobody talks about

1. **Layout:** staging cameras and blocking before any polish.
2. **Animation:** performance first, mechanics second.
3. **Lighting:** the stage where flat work suddenly feels like film.

Most of an episode's *quality* is decided in layout, long before the parts that
get the applause.

## Final frame

Compositing is where it all comes together, adding grain, grade, depth, and the
tiny imperfections that make a frame feel photographed rather than computed. When it
works, nobody notices the pipeline at all. That's the point.
