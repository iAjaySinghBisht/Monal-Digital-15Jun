After years of making content for young audiences, one thing is clear: kids are
the most honest audience on earth. They don't hate-watch. If it's boring, they
leave.

## Rhythm beats spectacle

The instinct is to go louder, with more colour, faster cuts, and bigger sound. It
backfires. What actually holds attention is **rhythm**: a reliable pattern of
setup and payoff that a child's brain can predict just enough to feel safe, and
be surprised just enough to stay curious.

## Safety is a feature

A young viewer needs to feel that the world of the show is *safe to return to*.
That doesn't mean nothing goes wrong; it means stakes always resolve. Predictable
emotional safety is why kids rewatch the same episode forty times.

## Designing the first 30 seconds

- Open on the character, not the logo.
- Establish the want immediately.
- Make a promise the episode then keeps.

Get those three right and retention takes care of itself. Everything else is
polish.
